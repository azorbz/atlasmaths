Absolutely Convergent Series

Introduction

A series, $\sum a_n$, is said to be absolutely convergent if $\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}|a_n|$. The most important property of absolutely convergent series is that if a series is absolutely convergent, it is also convergent (ie: $\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}|a_n|$ converges $\implies \sum_{n=1}^{\infty}a_n$ converges).

Note that the opposite does not hold.

Proof

Suppose that the infinite sum of $a_n$ is absolutely convergent.

Note that for all $n \in \mathbb{N}$, $0 \leq \left|a_n + |a_n|\right| \leq 2|a_n|$. Because of this, $$0 \leq \left|\sum_{k=1}^{n}(a_k + |a_k|)\right| \leq 2 \sum_{k=1}^{n}|a_k|$$ $$\implies 0 \leq \left|\sum_{k=1}^{n}a_k + \sum_{k=1}^{n}|a_k|\right| \leq 2 \sum_{k=1}^{n}|a_k|$$

Applying the Triangle Inequality gives, $$\implies 0 \leq \left|\sum_{k=1}^{n}a_k + \sum_{k=1}^{n}|a_k|\right| \leq \left|\sum_{k=1}^{n}a_k\right| + \sum_{k=1}^{n}|a_k| \leq 2 \sum_{k=1}^{n}|a_k|$$ $$\implies 0 \leq \left|\sum_{k=1}^{n}a_k\right|\leq \sum_{k=1}^{n}|a_k|$$ $$\implies 0 \leq \lim_{n\rightarrow\infty}\left|\sum_{k=1}^{n}a_k\right|\leq \lim_{n\rightarrow\infty}\sum_{k=1}^{n}|a_k|$$

We know that the right side of the inequality converges and thus $\left|\sum a_n\right|$ converges. Since $\sum a_n = \pm \left|\sum a_k\right|$, it must also converge.
