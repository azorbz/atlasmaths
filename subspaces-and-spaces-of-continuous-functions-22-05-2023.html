Subspaces And Spaces Of Continuous Functions

Subspaces

If $(X, ||\cdot||)$ is a Normed Space and $Y \subset X$ then $(Y, ||\cdot||)$ is another Normed Space, with $||\cdot||_{Y}: Y\rightarrow[0,\infty)$ the restriction of $||\cdot||$ to $Y$ defined by $||y||_Y = ||y||$, for all $y\in Y$.

Spaces Of Continuous Functions

The usual norm on $C([a,b])$ (defined as the space of continuous functions on the interval $[a,b]$) is the supremum (maximum) norm defined as $$||f||_{\infty}:=\sup_{x\in[a,b]}|f(x)|$$

A second example of a family of norms on this space is, for $p \in [1, \infty)$, $$||f||_{L^p}:=\left(\int_{a}^{b}|f(x)|^p dx\right)^{\frac{1}{p}}$$
